Buyer's Checklist

Before You Drive Out
Before You Go — Call First
Does it run and drive?Non-negotiable — get a clear yes/no before leaving home.
Does the AC blow cold?Say "cold" specifically — not just "does it have AC."
Clean title in hand?Avoid salvage, rebuilt, bonded, or missing titles.
Any frame rust?Frame rust = walk away. Surface body rust = negotiate hard.
Will you take $X?Always negotiate before making the drive.
At the Truck — Eyes On
Frame rails (flashlight under the truck)Rust, cracks, welds, prior repairs.
Cab corners and door bottomsThe #1 rust spot on 70s–80s trucks.
Lift the carpet — check the floorRust hides under carpet. Always look.
VIN: dash, door jamb, and title matchMismatched VINs = major red flag.
Body panel gaps even all aroundUneven gaps = prior collision.
Running Test
Cold start (ask before warming up)Tells you the most about engine health.
Exhaust color — blue / white / black?Blue = oil burning, white = coolant, black = rich.
AC cold at idle and at speed?Test both — some only work moving.
4WD engages — Hi and Lo?Both must engage cleanly.
Get a pre-purchase inspection$100–$150 mechanic fee is the best money you spend.
IH Scout — Extra Checks
Dana 44 axles front and rear?Strongest factory setup. Verify both.
345 V8 or 304 V8?345 = more torque, better for 4x4 use.
Check the rocker panels closelyNotorious rust spot on all Scout models.
Removable hardtop or soft top included?Adds $500–$2,000 to value.
Verify it's a Scout II (1971+), not Scout 800Parts availability differs significantly.
